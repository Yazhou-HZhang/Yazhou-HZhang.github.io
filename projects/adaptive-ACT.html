Adaptive-ACT: Adaptive Action Chunking for Manipulation

CS224R Final Project | 03/2025 – 06/2025

report  /  poster

Overview

Action Chunking with Transformers (ACT) enables temporally coherent control for manipulation by predicting fixed-length action chunks. However, a single fixed chunk size introduces a trade-off between temporal consistency and fine-grained responsiveness. In this project, we propose Adaptive-ACT, a method that dynamically selects action chunk lengths online to better handle varying task phases and contact conditions.

Method

We build on a pretrained ACT policy and introduce an adaptive chunk-length selector that operates on top of the frozen base policy. At each decision step, the selector chooses among multiple candidate chunk sizes, allowing the system to switch between longer chunks for smooth motion and shorter chunks for precise, contact-rich control.

  • A frozen ACT backbone trained with fixed-length action chunks.
  • A lightweight PPO-trained selector that outputs discrete chunk-length choices.
  • Residual corrections applied within each chunk to improve robustness under distribution shift.
Experimental Setup

We evaluate Adaptive-ACT in simulated manipulation tasks with varying dynamics and contact requirements. Tasks include free-space reaching, contact initiation, and fine manipulation, where responsiveness to unexpected interactions is critical.

Results
  • Adaptive-ACT consistently outperforms fixed-chunk ACT baselines across tasks requiring both smooth motion and precise control.
  • Dynamic chunk selection improves success rates in contact-rich phases without sacrificing overall trajectory coherence.
  • The selector learns intuitive strategies, favoring longer chunks in free motion and shorter chunks near contact.
Key Takeaways
  • Fixed action chunk sizes impose unnecessary constraints on manipulation policies.
  • Adaptive temporal abstraction provides a practical way to balance coherence and reactivity.
  • The approach is modular and can be applied to other chunked-action policy architectures.
Tools & Keywords

Action Chunking with Transformers (ACT), Reinforcement Learning, PPO, Temporal Abstraction, Manipulation, Policy Residuals
