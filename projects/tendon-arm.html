Salisbury Robotics Lab – Tendon Actuated Robotic Arm

Salisbury Lab | 01/2025 – 03/2025

demo

Overview

Developed a (n+1) DOFs tendon-actuated robotic arm with adjustable compliance, enabling adaptive interactions with objects of varying stiffness.

My Role

  • Position and Torque Control: Developed impedance-based control algorithms to regulate torque and stiffness, optimizing compliance in tendon-actuated joints for enhanced adaptability and performance.
  • Stiffness Perception: Implemented a stiffness perception algorithm that leverages motor position and torque control to infer material properties, enabling adaptive interaction with various surfaces.
  • Trajectory following: Developed and implemented a model for movement of a 3-DOF robotic arm, enabling smooth and precise motion execution.

Outcome & Impact

  • Achieved accurate position and torque control with self-adjustable compliance for both 1-DOF and 3-DOF systems.
  • Enabled real-time compliance adjustment based on external forces, and perceived object stiffness for both linear and stiffening material behavior.
