Mobile Manipulation for Semi-Autonomous Rice Cooking

CS225A: Experimental Robotics | 03/2025 – 06/2025

video / report

Overview

Developed a mobile manipulation system for semi-autonomous kitchen tasks, integrating perception, motion planning, and control to execute a complete rice-cooking workflow in a semi-structured environment.

Approach

  • Built a sim-to-real pipeline using MuJoCo and OpenSai to prototype perception and validate full-stack control before hardware deployment.
  • Designed a hierarchical finite-state machine coordinating perception, motion planning, and control for multi-stage tasks including container pickup, rice pouring, water dispensing, and cooker activation.
  • Integrated vision-guided manipulation using wrist-mounted RGB-D sensing and marker-based localization, with retry and recovery logic for robustness.
  • Implemented task-specific motion primitives combining Cartesian control, joint-space execution, and force-based interaction.

My Contributions

  • Implemented the full task-level state machine, including success checks, failure handling, and perception-triggered retries.
  • Developed and tuned manipulation primitives for grasping, pouring, and button pressing, ensuring stable contact transitions and repeatable execution.
  • Integrated perception outputs with motion planning and control to enable reliable end-to-end task execution on a mobile manipulator.

Outcome & Impact

  • Achieved centimeter-level positional accuracy and stable contact transitions during manipulation.
  • Demonstrated reliable execution of complex, multi-step kitchen tasks in a semi-structured setting.

Takeaways

  • Hierarchical task decomposition and explicit state management are critical for long-horizon mobile manipulation.
  • Simulation-first development significantly reduced hardware debugging time and improved safety.
