XPENG Robotics — Humanoid Dexterous Hand Manipulation

Embodied Intelligence Intern   |   Shenzhen, China   |   05/2025 – 09/2025

video (placeholder)  /  report (placeholder)

Summary

Worked on contact-rich manipulation for a humanoid dexterous hand, spanning compliance control, retargeting optimization, and learning-based manipulation pipelines. The focus was on improving robustness and iteration speed for dexterous behaviors under uncertain contact.

Technical Contributions
  • Admittance & compliance control: Implemented an admittance control framework to regulate contact forces during manipulation. Demonstrated safe interaction with fragile objects and compliant responses under rigid contact.
  • Retargeting optimization speedup: Replaced NLopt-based optimization with a direct QP formulation, reducing runtime from ~40 ms to 0.2 ms (≈200×). Built a similarity analysis workflow for rapid batch evaluation of retargeting outcomes.
  • Learning-based manipulation replication: Replicated state-of-the-art dexterous manipulation approaches including DexGen (PPO-based trajectory optimization with diffusion-based filtering) and ManipTrans (imitation learning combined with reinforcement learning for object-aware manipulation).
Outcome
  • Enabled robust, contact-aware manipulation behaviors across fragile and rigid interaction scenarios.
  • Achieved ≈200× optimization speedup, significantly improving responsiveness and iteration efficiency.
  • Established an evaluation workflow for fast pose/retargeting comparison at scale.
Tools & Keywords

Admittance Control, Compliance Control, Quadratic Programming (QP), Retargeting Optimization, PPO Reinforcement Learning, Diffusion Policies, Imitation Learning, Dexterous Manipulation
