XPENG Humanoid – Dexterous Hand Manipulation

XPENG Robotics | 06/2025 – 09/2025

Project Overview

  • Worked on a humanoid dexterous hand platform focusing on adaptive compliance control, high-speed retargeting optimization, and learning-based fine-grained manipulation, targeting robust interaction with fragile and rigid objects under uncertain contact.

My Role

  • Admittance & Compliance Control: Implemented an admittance control framework enabling adaptive regulation of contact forces. Demonstrated safe manipulation of fragile objects (e.g., balloons, chips) and compliant response under rigid contact.
  • Retargeting Optimization: Replaced NLopt-based optimization with a direct QP formulation, reducing runtime from ~40 ms to ~0.2 ms. Built a similarity analysis pipeline to support fast batch evaluation of dexterous retargeting results.
  • Learning-Based Manipulation: Replicated state-of-the-art dexterous manipulation methods, including DexGen (PPO-based trajectory optimization with diffusion filtering) and ManipTrans (imitation learning combined with reinforcement learning for object-aware manipulation).

Outcome & Impact

  • Enabled robust, contact-aware dexterous manipulation across diverse interaction scenarios.
  • Achieved 200× speedup in retargeting optimization, significantly improving iteration efficiency.
  • Established scalable evaluation supporting 30+ pose comparisons within minutes.
  • Validated learning-based manipulation pipelines applicable to real-world humanoid hand systems.
